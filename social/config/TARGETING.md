# Bestly Cloud — Targeting

## Channels

### Primary (own-content, auto-publish)
- **LinkedIn Company Page** — 1 post/day, business-buyer voice, technical-but-plain.
- (X/Twitter deliberately skipped — no account, $200/mo API pay wall not justified for a B2B seed audience.)

### Secondary (own posts, less frequent, human-checked)
- **Reddit** — new dedicated account (e.g. `u/bestly_cloud` — TBD). Occasional own posts in `r/selfhosted` "share your setup" / "I built this" threads. Follow each sub's self-promo policy strictly.

### Comment Scout targets (draft-only, human-posted)
- `r/selfhosted` — homelabbers escaping cloud lock-in
- `r/homelab` — enthusiasts running Proxmox/Unraid — adjacent buyers
- `r/devops` — SRE/platform folks evaluating on-prem vs. cloud
- `r/smallbusiness` — owners annoyed by per-seat SaaS
- `r/sysadmin` — IT pros managing office stacks
- `r/nextcloud` — direct-fit users, but be respectful (they already know the product)
- `r/msp` — managed service providers (potential resellers)

## Content themes (rotate — daily engine picks 1 per day, ~75% winners + 25% experiments)

1. **"SaaS math"** — real cost breakdown of common SaaS stacks (Google Workspace + Dropbox + Bitwarden + Notion + 1Password) vs. Bestly Cloud managed at $199/mo. Concrete numbers.
2. **"Own your infrastructure"** — anti-vendor-lock-in angle. Portability, exit clauses, data ownership.
3. **"Behind the box"** — dogfood updates: what runs on cloud.bestly.tech today, uptime, actual boring reliability posts.
4. **"Compliance-adjacent"** — HIPAA/GDPR/state-privacy considerations for small teams (posture, not certification).
5. **"Migration play-by-play"** — walkthrough of moving a specific SaaS off (e.g. off Google Workspace to Nextcloud + Talk).
6. **"Homelab vs. business"** — for the r/homelab crossover audience: at what team size does a managed appliance beat a DIY homelab.
7. **"The 3D device"** — visual/product post using the CloudScrollHero 3D animation. Ships the aesthetic.
8. **"Ask a boring question"** — technical Q&A hook — backups, restore times, DR, single-tenant vs multi.

## LinkedIn post format
- 1500 chars soft-cap, 1300 chars sweet spot.
- Hook line 1 (question or contrarian claim).
- 2–4 short paragraphs (2–3 sentences each).
- 1 concrete number if possible.
- End with: link to bestly.tech/cloud OR a direct question.
- No stack of emoji, no clickbait, no "🚀 exciting news."
- Attach: MP4 of the 3D device (see kit/videos/) OR a screenshot from the marketing site.

## Reddit post format
- Body-first, no self-promo in first 3 sentences.
- Speak like a real practitioner. Say "I built" or "I run" — never "our product."
- Include one concrete detail (spec, config, gotcha).
- Only link to bestly.tech if the sub allows AND the post is genuinely answering demand.

## Frequency
- LinkedIn: 1/day, weekday-biased (Tue/Wed/Thu perform best on LinkedIn organic).
- Reddit own posts: max 1-2/week per sub, respect self-promo ratios (Reddit rule of thumb: 90% helping others, 10% own).
- Reddit comments: 5–15/day, human-posted, spaced out.

## Hashtags
- LinkedIn: `#selfhosted #nextcloud #privacy #smallbusiness #cloud #devops` (2–4 per post max).
- Reddit: **no hashtags** (Reddit ignores them + they signal amateur).

## Excluded / handle with care
- `r/sysadmin` — very strict about vendor posts. Comments only, never own posts.
- `r/privacy` — same. Comments only, and mostly no-mention pure-help.
- Don't ever comment on a competitor's ads or paid posts. Only organic threads.
