## 7. Objection handling

### "We already use [Google Workspace / Microsoft 365] and it's fine."

> Totally fair. Most of our customers came from there too — we don't replace it on day one; we run alongside until your team trusts the new stack. The real question is what happens at your next renewal: GWS is going up 17% in 2026 for Business Standard. If you're at 80 seats, that's an extra $1,300/month forever. Bestly's bill doesn't change.

### "What about uptime? If the box dies, we die."

> Two answers. One: 99.9% average across our deployed fleet, which is better than the cloud vendors quote in their SLAs (and they only credit you for the downtime, they don't fix your day). Two: we ship a replication option for $5,500 — second box in the same office (or a satellite office) takes over within 30 seconds if the primary dies. We test failover quarterly during the support relationship.

### "My team will never learn a new tool."

> They won't have to learn most of it. Talk looks like Zoom, Files looks like Drive, Docs looks like Google Docs, Mail looks like Gmail. The AI Assistant is a chat box. The pieces that are actually different — password vault, calendar sharing — are the same UX as 1Password and Google Calendar. We do a 90-minute onboarding session with everyone in the first week and full ticket support for 90 days after.

### "What if we want to leave?"

> The data is yours. Files, Mail, Calendar, Talk history — all standard formats (eml, CalDAV, ical, mbox), exportable any time, no contractual lock-in. The hardware is also yours; we don't lease it. If you want to leave you walk away with everything, the same way you'd walk away with your filing cabinet.

### "Isn't running your own server a security risk?"

> The opposite is usually true. You control the patches, the access logs, the encryption keys, and the network rules. The big-cloud risk model is "we hope they don't get breached and we hope they tell us when they do." The on-prem risk model is "we know exactly who's on the system at all times." We harden the install — Tailscale-only access, Fail2Ban, automatic security updates, encrypted disk at rest. You can audit it any time.

### "We need SSO / SCIM / SAML."

> Built in. SAML, OIDC, and LDAP all supported. SCIM for user provisioning. The IT lead in your discovery call will walk through it with our team. If you have Okta or JumpCloud today, it's a 30-minute setup.

### "What about compliance — HIPAA / SOC 2 / GDPR?"

> HIPAA: deployment can be configured for HIPAA workloads with a signed BAA; the encryption-at-rest + access-log requirements are met out of the box. SOC 2: we're working toward Type 1 attestation on the Bestly-managed support tier; the underlying stack is built on auditable open-source components (Nextcloud, Postgres, Talk HPB) that have been independently penetration-tested. GDPR: data never leaves your country, period — that's the whole point.

### "The AI features in [vendor] are getting better every month. Why bet on a custom box?"

> Because the AI features in Bestly's stack are also getting better every month — and they're switchable. Today you can swap between Claude Sonnet 4.5, GPT-5, Gemini Pro, and 350+ other models on a per-conversation basis from a dropdown. When the next big model launches in Q4, you turn it on the same day. You're not locked to one vendor's roadmap, and the model never trains on your prompts.

### "Show me the price." (the one you'll hear most)

> Hardware: $6,500 for ≤50 users, $11,500 for ≤100, $17,500 for ≤200, +$5,500 per 100 beyond. Optional managed support: $500/mo or self-managed free. Local AI: $0. BYOK API costs: about $30–$100/mo at typical usage. That's it. Run the live calculator at bestly.tech/cloud with your seat count and current stack — it'll show you the three-year delta in real time.

### "We don't have anyone to set this up."

> That's our job. The standard install includes discovery call, hardware delivery, on-site or remote setup (90 min typical), data migration from your current stack (Drive, Gmail, calendars, contacts), team training, and 90 days of post-install support. You're not picking up a screwdriver.

### "We tried open-source self-hosted before and it was a nightmare."

> So did we, which is why we exist. The stack you're describing was probably an admin in a basement reading wiki pages. Bestly is the productization: pre-configured hardware, pre-hardened security, pre-integrated apps, professional install, ongoing managed updates if you want them. Same software ethos, none of the DIY pain.

## 9. Metrics worth tracking

Build a single weekly scorecard. Don't over-instrument; these 8 numbers tell the story:

1. **Calculator completion rate** — % of /cloud visitors who move the slider AND check at least one service. Target: >40%.
2. **Cold-email reply rate** — % of sends that get any human reply. Target: 8–12% for ICP 1, 15–25% for ICP 2 (warmer audience), 5–8% for ICP 3.
3. **Demo-book rate** — % of replies that convert to a scheduled call. Target: 30%.
4. **Demo-to-close rate** — % of held demos that become deals within 60 days. Target: 20% in year one, 35% in year two as testimonials accumulate.
5. **CAC per ICP** — total spend / closed deals. Should not exceed 25% of first-year ACV.
6. **Time-to-close** — days from first touch to signed SOW. Track per ICP — expect ICP 1 = 60 days, ICP 2 = 14 days, ICP 3 = 30 days.
7. **Partner-sourced pipeline** — % of opp value originating from a referrer/affiliate vs. cold. Healthy goal: 25% by Q2, 40% by Q4.
8. **Net Promoter Score at 90-day-live** — survey every customer 90 days after Stage 8 completion. Aim for 50+.
